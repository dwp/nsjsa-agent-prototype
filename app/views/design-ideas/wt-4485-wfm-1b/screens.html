Workflow Management 1b: Iteration 1

Hypothesis 1: Pausing a claim

We believe that letting Service Centre agents choose from a list of pause reasons and letting them set an action by date will achieve that agents have the ability to record the pause reason on the system without having to maintain manual records.

We will know this when we test a prototype and users tell us that they know when to select each pause reason and their associated due dates without having to make a manual record elsewhere except on JSAPS.

Hypothesis 2: Claim history

We believe that displaying the paused reason in the timeline on the customer statement will achieve that agents can see at a glance where the claim is at and what action is outstanding.

We will know this when we test a prototype and users tell us the pause reason gives a starting point for working on the claim and they would go to JSAPS for detailed claim notes.

Hypothesis 3: Pause list

We believe that showing a list of paused claims by agent will achieve that that Service Centre agents can prioritise which paused claim to look at next and what they need to do with it.

We will know this when we test a prototype and users tell us the list helps them selecting the right paused claim that is due to be looked at next.

Hypothesis 4: Reassigning claims

We believe that giving Service Centre agents the ability to search for paused claims by staff number on the paused list will achieve that agents can easily pick up a colleague's claim without having to check manual records.

We will know this when we test a prototype and users tell us that the search by staff number feature gives them an overview of a colleague's outstanding paused claims and it helps them to identify the right claim to pick up and what to do to take ownership of that claim.
